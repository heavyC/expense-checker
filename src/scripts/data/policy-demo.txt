Corporate Travel and Expense Policy

General Principles
- All business expenses must be reasonable, necessary, and directly related to company business.
- Employees are expected to exercise the same care in incurring expenses as they would with their own funds.
- All expenses must be submitted within 30 days of being incurred.
- Expenses submitted more than 90 days after the transaction date will not be reimbursed.
- All claims must be accompanied by itemized receipts for expenses over $25.
- Falsifying expense reports is grounds for immediate termination.

Air Travel
- Employees must book economy class for flights under 6 hours.
- Business class is permitted for international flights exceeding 6 hours in the air.
- Flights must be booked at least 14 days in advance whenever possible.
- Employees should select the lowest logical fare available within a 2-hour window of the preferred departure time.
- Personal travel combined with business trips is allowed, but the company will only reimburse the cost equivalent to the business-only itinerary.
- Baggage fees for one checked bag are reimbursable.
- Airline rewards points, miles, or upgrades earned during business travel belong to the employee.

Lodging
- Hotel accommodations should not exceed $250 per night in major metropolitan areas and $175 per night in other locations.
- Employees should book standard rooms; suite upgrades are not reimbursable unless required for client meetings.
- Hotel incidentals such as room service, minibar charges, and movie rentals are not reimbursable.
- Hotel internet fees required for business purposes are reimbursable with documentation.

Ground Transportation
- Rideshare services (Uber, Lyft) and taxis are reimbursable for business travel.
- Rental cars should be mid-size or smaller unless transporting equipment or multiple employees.
- Personal vehicle mileage is reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage rate.
- Parking and toll fees are reimbursable with receipts.
- Public transportation is encouraged when practical and is fully reimbursable.

Meals and Entertainment
- Meal expenses while traveling are reimbursable up to $75 per day, including tips.
- Itemized receipts are required for all meals over $25.
- Alcohol is not reimbursable except when entertaining clients, and is limited to two drinks per person.
- Client entertainment expenses require the names and companies of all attendees and a description of the business purpose.
- Team meals and celebrations require manager pre-approval and are capped at $50 per person.
- Tips should not exceed 20% of the pre-tax bill.

Office and Equipment
- Home office equipment purchases over $100 require manager approval prior to purchase.
- Standard office supplies (pens, notebooks, etc.) are reimbursable without pre-approval up to $50 per month.
- Software subscriptions and SaaS tools must be approved by IT and procurement before purchase.
- Mobile phone reimbursement for business use is capped at $50 per month and requires submission of the phone bill.

Approval and Submission
- All expense reports must be submitted through the company expense management system.
- Expenses under $25 do not require a receipt but must include a description of the expense.
- Expenses between $25 and $500 require manager approval.
- Expenses over $500 require both manager and finance director approval.
- Expense reports must include a valid cost center and project code.
- Reimbursements are processed within 10 business days of approval.

Non-Reimbursable Expenses
- Personal entertainment, including movies, gym memberships, and spa services.
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, and other fines.
- Personal travel insurance.
- Clothing, except for required uniforms or safety equipment.
- Childcare or pet care services.
- Costs related to lost personal property.

International Travel
- Employees traveling internationally must notify the travel and security team at least 7 days before departure.
- Currency conversion fees on company credit cards are reimbursable.
- Visa fees required for business travel are reimbursable with documentation.
- Per diem rates for international travel follow the published country-specific rates on the finance portal.

Corporate Credit Cards
- Corporate credit cards must only be used for approved business expenses.
- Personal charges on a corporate card must be reimbursed to the company within 5 business days.
- Lost or stolen corporate cards must be reported to the finance team within 24 hours.
- Cardholders must reconcile all transactions monthly through the expense system.
